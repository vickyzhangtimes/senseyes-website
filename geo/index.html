What is GEO

What is GEO

GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is a brand visibility management methodology for large language models such as ChatGPT, DeepSeek, Doubao, and Gemini.

Unlike traditional SEO, which ranks web pages on search results pages, GEO focuses on: whether your brand is included as a candidate when AI generates answers, whether your content is cited, and whether your product is recommended.

The SEO Era

User searches → Views page rankings → Clicks through → Compares one by one

The GEO Era

User asks → AI generates answer → Direct recommendation → One-step decision

GEO Visibility Model

AI Visibility Scoring Model: L0 — L3

Measuring your brand's "decision influence" in AI responses — not just mention counts, but a four-tier progression from invisible to recommended.

L0

Invisible

Your brand is completely absent from AI responses. Users ask relevant questions, but your name never appears.

Decision Influence 0%
L1

Recognized

Your brand is mentioned but only as a name in a list — no content is cited, unable to influence user decisions.

Decision Influence ~20%
L2

Cited

AI cites your content, data, or viewpoints as evidence when composing answers. You begin to participate in answer generation.

Decision Influence ~60%
L3

Recommended

AI explicitly recommends you in "how to choose" scenarios. You become the top choice in the user's decision path.

Decision Influence 100%
GEO Methodology

The Complete GEO Cycle: From Audit to Continuous Optimization

Six stages form a complete business loop from "understanding your position" to "continuous improvement," each with clear inputs, outputs, and decision criteria.

01

AI Visibility Audit

Test how AI currently identifies, cites, and recommends your brand using real questions. Establish baselines and determine priority intervention points.

  • Based on real questions, not abstract metrics
  • Multi-platform parallel observation
  • Distinguish "recognized" from "recommended"
02

User Decision Map

Map the key decision nodes and trigger questions from awareness to selection, identifying the question entry points that truly influence recommendations.

  • Awareness, comparison, filtering, decision stages
  • Locate high-impact question scenarios
  • Align question chains with recommendation chains
03

Question Asset Framework

Consolidate high-frequency, scenario-based, comparative, and long-tail questions into a structured matrix — the content foundation for the AI era.

  • Reduce dependency on ad-hoc content production
  • Turn questions themselves into long-term assets
  • Provide standard test questions for continuous testing
04

Answer Engineering

Design AI-friendly answer structures around key questions, making content easier to understand, cite, and compare.

  • Shift from marketing copy to answer architecture
  • Strengthen comparable data fields
  • Improve answer absorption efficiency
05

Source Authority System

Build a trust network of official sites, case studies, knowledge pages, and external authoritative content — grounding recommendations on verifiable trust signals.

  • Official content provides authority
  • External content provides interpretive power
  • From being seen to being trusted
06

Continuous Monitoring

Continuously monitor changes in visibility, citation, and recommendation across AI platforms, turning insights into actionable iteration steps.

  • Focus on change, not one-time results
  • Integrate monitoring into business rhythm
  • Form a long-term strategic feedback loop
